Air Quality in the United States
  • Since 2000 air quality monitoring has become an important data point for making planning decisions for transportation planning, sustainable economic growth and overall emergency management planning.
  • The Community Multiscale Air Quality Modeling System [CMAQ] is promoted and managed by the EPA to help federal, state, local and community agencies access models that represent:
  • Atmospheric and weather activities
  • Emisson models that represent man-made and naturally occuring contributions to the atmosphere
  • Use air chemistry-transport model[s] to predict the atmospheric fate of air pollutants under varying conditions
